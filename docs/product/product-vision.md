# Product Vision

## Problem
People with Type 1 diabetes repeatedly translate food into carbohydrates under time pressure. Existing tools are either locally narrow, overloaded with unreliable crowd data, difficult to use during a real meal, or unsafe when they blur food estimation with insulin advice.

## Vision
T1Dine makes meal understanding fast, transparent, local, and reusable. It helps a user answer:
1. What am I eating?
2. How much carbohydrate and related nutrition is in this meal?
3. How reliable is the estimate?
4. What data and settings were used in any optional calculation?

## Principles
- Food first, calculation second.
- One global model, local market data.
- Confidence and provenance are visible.
- Offline-first for core meal tasks.
- Caregiver-aware without removing user autonomy.
- AI accelerates input but never hides uncertainty.
- Clinical functionality is isolated and independently governed.

## Success measures
- Median time to create a repeated meal.
- Percentage of meals created from verified or previously confirmed data.
- Search success by country and language.
- User correction rate after barcode, OCR, or photo assistance.
- Sync reliability and offline completion rate.
- Accessibility task completion.
- Safety-gate rejection and stale-input detection rates for Dose Assist.

Do not use time saved as the only success metric. Faster wrong data is not value.
